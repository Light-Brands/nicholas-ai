# My story from 0-28 yrs

## **Godzillionnaire**

Your tests become your testimony, your trials become your triumphs, your mess becomes your message, and a miracle to someone else.

It was a beautiful hot and sunny morning. As I was walking in the forest, the sun was creating these beautiful beams of light piercing through the leaves and creating this wonderful tapestry mixed with the melody of the birds singing with joy.

Suddenly, I came to this open field, with a beautiful stream coming down the mountain. I started to walk towards the water. As I looked far away, there was a bench alongside the stream, and someone was sitting there looking at the scenery. Something inside invited me to walk toward the bench.

As I came closer, I could tell it was a child sitting down. When I arrived a few feet away, I was completely in shock. The child sitting on that bench was me at the age of eight years old.

Through his innocent big blue eyes, he looked at me. Tears came down his cheeks. He reached out his hand and said:

“Can you promise to love me, care for me, protect me, and activate me in becoming everything God created me to be for the rest of your life?”

I woke up. I was thirty-nine years old.

---

I was born on the 15th of December, 1980\. I was blessed to have two amazing parents who loved me unconditionally. My first memories are infused with peace, joy, and harmony.

My grandmother on my Dad’s side raised me until the age of four years old while my mother was the assistant director of the National Bank and my Dad was teaching ski and bartending. During that time, my grandmother raised me with excellence, love, peace, and joy. The heart of gold and goodness of this lady left a mark in my soul that will never be erased.

While I was approaching four years old, my mother had a spiritual awakening. Her life would forever be transformed. She met God, her Creator and Father. He spoke to her and invited her on a new journey, a journey that would change her destiny. He invited her to sacrifice her professional career and come home to raise her son in God’s way. She accepted the invitation.

My Dad was in unbelief. He couldn’t understand what was happening to my mother and was really disturbed by the transformation in her life. My mother would bring me to Bible studies each Friday night and Sundays. She would pray and have faith that what God united, He would not separate.

At an early age, I accepted Jesus as my Lord and Savior into my life.

One night, my Dad was having a late dinner. I was playing with my toys on the kitchen floor. I stood up, sat on a chair beside him, looked at him straight in his eyes, and said to him:

“If you don’t accept Jesus as your Lord and Savior in your heart, you will go to hell, but if you do, you will come to heaven with Mommy and me.”

I turned around, went back to playing with my toys, and left my Dad astonished, digesting these few powerful words that came out of his little boy’s heart.

The following Sunday, he came with us to church and made the greatest decision of his life. He accepted Jesus into his heart.

## **PART 2 — Family Transformation, Faith, and Childhood Formation**

---

From that moment, the inner and outer dynamic of our family was completely changed. My Dad changed careers. He left behind the nightlife and became a car salesman. Financially, it had a major impact. Only one salary was coming in, and it was much leaner than the previous two jobs my Dad used to work.

What still amazes me today is the fact that we never lived poor-minded. We trusted God. Our needs were always met, and we lived in an abundance of love, peace, and joy.

I remember one time at the end of the month when my parents were short financially. They did not have enough to cover the mortgage and gas for my Dad to go to work. They prayed fiercely. The next day, someone came to our house and handed an envelope to my Dad. He said he felt in his heart to bless us with a specific amount. The amount of the check was exactly what was needed to cover the mortgage, and an extra twenty dollars covered the gas.

As long as I can remember, our life was constantly covered by God’s supernatural fingerprints.

---

My upbringing was fulfilled by activities and sports. I didn’t have any siblings. My Dad was my best friend. He would play with me for hours. My mother was the wild one. She brought me to do crazy activities and always empowered and motivated me to push my limits.

We didn’t have a television at home. We used our creativity to build treehouses, a hockey rink in our backyard during winter, skateboarding ramps, and excursions in the forest playing GI Joe. We went swimming, played tennis, soccer, karate, rock climbing, and many other sports.

My Dad put me in a hockey league where I excelled. That is when the entrepreneurial spirit came alive in me. I would go back home and invite friends to come to our house on Saturday mornings to learn how to play hockey. I would teach them what I learned during practice and charged them twenty-five cents per lesson. My first business was born.

---

We would also spend March break and two weeks during the summer at my grandmother’s house by Blue Sea Lake with my cousins. We built a cabin in the woods where we would sleep and enjoy my Dad playing guitar while the family sang around a campfire.

Skidoo, four-wheelers, dirt bikes, cars, fishing boats — these were part of our everyday activities. We were young, full of life, and enjoying every minute.

We also traveled every year to church conferences in Montreal, the Laurentians, and Thunder Bay, camping along the way and enjoying the beautiful scenery.

---

Our home welcomed many people who stayed with us for a time — friends, family, and my grandmother on my mom’s side. She inspires me to this day. She was the mother of fifteen children, lived in poverty, lost her husband and a son to suicide, and worked three jobs.

I never heard her complain. She sang all the time, and she had a smile that lit up the world.

My parents always opened the door of our home and their hearts to anyone in need. They loved people unconditionally, and each one left inspired and impacted by the atmosphere of love, peace, and joy inhabiting our home.

I realized at a very young age that many people have a house, but very few have a home. We had a home.

---

Many friends and family members gave their lives to Jesus because they saw more than they heard the fruits of transformation in our life and home.

Both my grandmothers became prayer warriors. When they left to be with Jesus, we felt the void of their answered prayers in our life.

As a family, we took time each morning and each night to read the Bible, pray, worship, and give grace before each meal.

My mother would come to my bedside every night, and we would recite three verses from the Bible. I remember how they uplifted my spirit as a little boy, and I remember them to this day.

From an early age, I learned to be grateful for the abundance of life and even the small things that made us so happy.

---

The gift of communication came to me at a young age. I would sit my parents and grandmother in a circle, and with the help of a whiteboard, I would teach and preach what I learned during Bible studies at church.

This was the beginning of my public speaking.

To understand my battle, you must understand my roots.

I was raised in a strict, closed-brethren Christian assembly where church was our whole life. We had no TV, no radio, no access to culture beyond our walls. I was overprotected and taught to fear God more than love Him. I learned rules, not relationship; doctrine, not connection; fear, not grace.

And in that same church, my innocence was stolen.

An older boy abused me for three years — from age eight to eleven — twisting my trust, silencing my voice, and fracturing my sense of self.

---

My eagerness to connect with God was still at an all-time high. I had dreams and visions of traveling the world, preaching Jesus, and becoming a missionary. In my young mind, I tried to connect my entrepreneurial spirit with a calling to ministry. I always hated the poverty mindset and believed there would be a way to create for-profit businesses to support ministry.

I remember the first time I heard that it was possible to become more than a millionaire — that someone could become a billionaire. Something shifted in my spirit.

The following Sunday at church, an older lady asked me, “Nicholas, what do you want to become when you grow older?”

I responded without hesitation, “Madame, I will become a billionaire.”

She replied, “Well, you know, Nicholas, that money is the root of all evil?”

I responded, “No, Madame. The love of money is the root of all evil. How can I bless millions of people if I don’t make billions of dollars?”

No one answered. That was the end of the conversation. I was twelve years old.

---

The church we attended had amazing Bible studies but was also very religious at its core and stood on many doctrines that I do not believe today reflect the will of God for our lives.

I would share the dreams and visions I had about entrepreneurship and life with church members and was always met with similar responses — that these dreams were not from God but from the world.

This suppression of what I felt was my purpose, passion, and desire made me very confused.

---

At fourteen years old, I met a beautiful young lady. I felt completely in love. I had never felt this type of emotion before. I couldn’t get her out of my mind.

We started going out, and my life became a love story. She knew I was different because of my upbringing and accepted me just as I was.

Nothing else mattered. I loved her with all my heart and believed she would become my wife and that we would live happily ever after.

Love made me blind. I started making choices that went against my beliefs and values.

One night, I went to a friend’s party with my girlfriend and tasted alcohol for the first time. Suddenly, all my insecurities, differences, and fears disappeared. For the first time in my life, I felt like I was part of the tribe.

Not long after, I gave her my virginity, convincing myself she was the one.

I started lying and manipulating my parents to make them believe we were only friends.

---

Nine months into our relationship, one evening she told me she didn’t love me anymore and was breaking up with me.

My world shattered.

Never before had I felt real suffering and pain like this. I struggled to breathe. I became deeply depressed and didn’t want to live.

I asked myself how a good God could allow His child to suffer so much. Why was life so cruel?

Pain is inevitable — but suffering is a choice.

That day, I chose to suffer.

I made a vow: I would never let myself fall in love again. I would never let myself suffer like this again.

Love meant suffering, and suffering became my greatest fear.

Worse, I turned my back on God and crowned myself my own god.

## **PART 4 — Anger, Identity Collapse, and the Second Covenant**

---

Around that time, someone went to the car dealership where my Dad worked. He asked the receptionist to speak to Jeff Courchesne. When my Dad showed up, he saw a teenage boy standing beside the reception desk.

My Dad said to him, “Hello, how may I help you?”

The young boy replied, “Hello, Dad.”

Before giving his life to Jesus, my Dad had been living a double life. He had been completely truthful with my mother about his affairs and the possibility that he may have other children. My mother forgave him wholeheartedly and never brought the subject back.

She explained, “Who am I not to forgive? I have been completely forgiven by Jesus, and my responsibility is to extend that forgiveness toward my husband and love toward that young boy.”

My Dad and my half-brother tried to connect at that time, but it was not meant to be. Something always seemed to come up that pushed back the possibility of connection until they eventually lost contact.

When I learned about my half-brother and my Dad’s affair, I became extremely angry. It fueled resentment, anxiety, and suffering to a whole new level.

I started questioning how my pain and hurt could make me shift internally so rapidly, forgetting all of my values, mindset, and beliefs.

Was the suppression of my dreams and visions, combined with suffering and confusion, the beginning of a fast downhill spiral toward worldly sedation and darkness?

It was.

---

I remember telling myself, “If this is God, then I will be my own god.”

Wrong choices followed wrong choices to numb the pain. I started hanging out with the wrong crowd. I experienced many drugs mixed with alcohol. Partying like rock stars became normal. I had sex with many young girls and treated them with no respect, despite their emotions.

The entrepreneur in me quickly connected the dots, and without hesitation, I became a drug dealer.

My parents were being called to school weekly because of my behavior. Stealing, fighting, hurting people, having people overdose and potentially die because of the drugs we were selling — this became part of the lifestyle surrounding my rise toward becoming the king of the jungle.

---

During my fourth year of high school, we participated in an exchange trip with another school in Costa Rica. The goal was to study their culture and learn Spanish.

It took less than a week for the teachers to realize that I was influencing other students to escape the houses we were living in to go clubbing and get high on drugs and alcohol.

They made the decision to send me back home one week before the end of the trip.

My parents picked me up from the airport in Montreal. Not one word was spoken during the two-hour drive back home to Gatineau.

When I entered the house, there was one pound of marijuana waiting for me on the kitchen table.

I was in shock. How could they have found it? It was so well hidden.

My parents looked at me with despair. Sadness, hurt, and pain embodied their entire being.

---

The next day, my Dad sat me down and said:

“Son, I love you, but you have two choices. You need to change your lifestyle and live under the values of this house, or you can continue in your ways, but you will have to leave our home.”

I went into my room, packed my bags, walked back through the kitchen, and stopped between the two exit doors.

I turned around and looked at my Dad. He was standing by the counter, devastated. Love, anger, sadness, and pain were written all over his face.

Looking straight into his eyes, I said:

“One day you will hear about Nicholas Courchesne. I will succeed no matter what. Watch me.”

I turned around and left home, just like the prodigal son.

That day, I made a second covenant with myself.

I was going to become famous, rich, successful, and important. I was going to prove to my Dad — for all the wrong reasons — that even if I disobeyed and dishonored him, I was going to make it big.

Whatever needed to be done to reach the summit of worldly, illusional success, I was ready to do it.

## **PART 5 — Police Pressure, Criminal Record, and Entry into the Nightlife**

---

Much tension started happening with the school and the police. They knew about my business and were preparing to catch me in action. My grades started to go downhill, and because of the situation that happened in Costa Rica, the direction of the school made the decision to expel me from the international program.

I finished my last year of high school in the general program instead and did not graduate with honors from the international program.

The police busted me at school, but by grace, on that day I had no drugs on me.

A small voice inside of me kept telling me to walk away from the drug business — that my days were numbered and that prison would become my next home if I didn’t make a drastic decision.

I woke up the next morning and made the ultimate decision. I was done. I liquidated everything I had and never looked back.

The police were not happy, but they had their fair share when I got caught one day stealing rims from a car in the parking lot of a mall. They didn’t miss the opportunity to charge me with theft and falsely charge me with resisting arrest and being violent.

The judge had no mercy. I was convicted of three felonies and had to face the consequences of being labeled with a criminal record at the age of sixteen years old.

---

During that time, I had very unhealthy relationships with girls my age and even with a woman who was ten years older than me. She opened the world of sexual immorality to me at a whole new level.

Because of the covenant I had made with myself, as soon as I started feeling any type of emotion toward someone, I would do everything in my power to destroy the relationship.

---

In the middle of this dark episode, I met an angel. Her name was Nancy. She was beautiful inside and out, and just like my grandmother, her smile would light up the world.

We started going out, and for the first time, I found someone who could see past the persona and reveal the heart of the little boy hidden deep inside of me behind many masks and the fake identity I had spent so much time and energy creating.

It didn’t take long for her to realize that love gives freedom.

She said to me, “Nicholas, I really love you, but I believe you need to continue your journey by yourself and discover what you are looking for on your own.”

She explained that what she had fallen in love with was my heart.

How could she see my heart? I had left it many years ago, broken into pieces on the path of life.

I was cold and frozen. It felt like I didn’t have a heart anymore.

---

Her upbringing was the opposite of mine. Her mother was an alcoholic. Her parents separated when she was very young. She left home at fifteen years old, working two jobs and taking responsibility for her brother because they were ready to commit suicide if they didn’t escape the broken environment they lived in.

Through all these challenges, she had grandparents who loved her, believed in her, empowered her, and introduced her to Jesus.

She prayed fiercely and asked God, “When will I be happy?”

There was something different about Nancy. We connected at the spirit and soul level.

When we decided to go our own ways, she looked directly into my eyes, straight to my soul, and said, “One day I will marry you.”

I was in shock.

Who would want to marry someone as crazy as me after getting to know me?

Was she the crazy one, or was she seeing something I couldn’t see about myself?

We continued to be friends. We became best friends and confidants.

---

I started hanging out with a different crowd of friends. They were not in the drug business, but they were deep in the nightlife scene.

I also went to college, but I faced many challenges when I realized I wouldn’t be able to succeed without studying. This was no longer high school.

At the same time, I was trying to be a noble citizen by working a normal job at minimum wage. The drop in income was brutal. I had made thousands of dollars per week in my previous business, and now I barely made one thousand dollars per month working nonstop.

I hated being told what to do and when to do it.

A few weeks into my third job, I was fired once again.

I decided to drop out of school.

---

I started working in the nightlife scene in Toronto.

I became a creature of the night — darkness and obscurity.

Sex, drugs, and rock and roll.

No limits.  
No boundaries.

I lived by night, slept by day, and repeated the cycle over and over again.

I was making crazy money indulging myself in the ephemeral, dark pleasures of this world.

## **PART 6 — New York, Cocaine, and the Illusion of Invincibility**

---

A friend explained to me that he had contacts in New York in the nightlife scene. He said that guys were making three to four times more money in one night than what we were making in Toronto.

A few weeks later, at the age of eighteen years old, I was on a plane heading to the Big Apple in pursuit of fame, money, and success.

This is when the true downfall into hell started.

New York — the city that never sleeps.

This is where I met a new friend called cocaine.

It was completely normal. Everyone was sniffing coke the same way they would take a drink. It was socially acceptable.

This friend made me feel like Superman. Fearless.

It boosted my ego. I felt like I was on top of the world.

I was extremely good-looking, fit, full of charm, highly manipulative, and knew how to work the game better than most.

I influenced many friends back home to follow me into the pit of hell.

I made more money than I could have ever dreamed of at my age, living like a rock star.

Five-star restaurants.  
 VIP access to the best clubs in the city.  
 A buffet of beautiful girls ready to do whatever it took to be part of the glamour and have access to more drugs.

All of this came at the expense of prostituting my values to make more money.

---

Through all of this, even though I lied to my parents about my lifestyle — and they knew the truth — they never disowned me.

On the contrary, they loved me unconditionally and prayed for me intensively.

My mother kept repeating a verse from the book of Proverbs, chapter 22, verse 6:

“Train up a child in the way he should go, teaching him to seek God’s wisdom and will for his abilities and talents, and even when he is old, he will not depart from it.”

She would add that she believed in her heart that God would bring me back onto the right path. She heard Him clearly when He told her to come home and raise me in God’s ways.

“You have a great calling on your life, Nicholas,” she would say. “I didn’t sacrifice my professional career for you not to accomplish the destiny God created for you before the foundation of time.”

---

Even in the midst of this lifestyle, I could tell something was different about me compared to my friends.

After hard nights of partying, they would feel the aftermath of drugs and alcohol the next day.

For me, it was much worse.

My soul would twist in agony, to the point of feeling like demons were battling for my life.

A voice inside me kept telling me that I was running away from my destiny and making cruel decisions that could cost me my life.

I hated that voice and those feelings.

I used more drugs and alcohol to sedate and silence them.

What I realize today is that I was fighting the nature of God within me — twenty-four hours a day, seven days a week, three hundred sixty-five days a year.

---

During the next three years, I traveled back and forth between New York and Canada, transferring large amounts of money and beginning to invest in real estate.

I also traveled to cities around the world — Tokyo, Dallas, Los Angeles, and Miami — working in the nightlife scene.

By the age of twenty-one years old, I had “made it.”

I had made more than one million dollars.

I had several lucrative investments.

I was self-made.

---

The truth was that I was completely empty inside.

Alone.  
Scared.  
Wanting more.

This was not how it was supposed to feel.

I was supposed to feel fulfilled, happy, complete.

I had access to all the pleasure this world could offer, yet my soul was still in agony.

I bought a brand-new Corvette of the year and drove around town like the king of the hill.

I owned a beautiful penthouse in a trendy part of Canada and rented another penthouse in New York on 45th Street and 10th Avenue in Hell’s Kitchen.

All of it was driven by the pursuit of acceptance and approval.

## **PART 7 — September 11th, Awakening, and Remaining in New York**

---

On September 11th, 2001, I was traveling in a taxi with a friend toward downtown New York. It was early in the morning. The taxi driver told us that one of the Twin Tower buildings had been struck by a plane. We thought he was insane. Many people in the city are mentally impaired.

A few seconds later, we heard a loud noise above our heads. The driver turned on the radio, and the host screamed with a trembling voice that the second tower had just been hit and that the United States of America was under attack.

We were in complete disbelief.

The taxi dropped us off, and as we looked up, we saw the most devastating scene of our lives. Black smoke covered the sky. People were jumping from the towers to their own death.

We started walking toward the Twin Towers.

My friend, who was from the Middle East, said to me, “We should turn around. I’m telling you, they probably have bombs on the planes, and these towers will come down.”

I replied, “That’s impossible. These are the Twin Towers. You’re crazy.”

Six blocks from the towers, the inevitable happened.

The first tower came crashing down.

Thick clouds of smoke traveled down the streets and avenues. We turned around and started running for our lives.

A few blocks down, covered in ashes, we caught our breath and looked back. We could see the face of evil smiling at the devastating destruction.

Later that afternoon, while walking in Times Square, we could hear the sound of birds flying.

What in God’s name had just happened?

Why was I there?

Did God have a message for me?

---

The following months were extremely challenging. The city felt dead. Work was hard to find. Passing immigration became extremely complex with the new security measures, and transferring money from the United States to Canada became almost impossible.

We made the decision to move to New York and stay there most of the time, returning to Canada only for the holidays.

---

I stayed in constant communication with Nancy throughout the years. When I decided to move permanently to New York, she rented my penthouse in Canada.

I had a girlfriend in Toronto, but I still invited her to come visit me in New York.

Nancy was also working in the nightlife scene. She had to support her brothers financially for some time, and she also chose the easy way of making money.

She fell in love with New York, found work easily, and decided to move in with me.

We were still just friends at that time.

---

Her love for me was unchanged. She treated me like a king. She knew what I loved and overwhelmed me with the fine things in life.

She wanted to see me happy.

I manipulated her — pushing her limits and stepping on her values, especially in regard to partying and fulfilling my dark sexual desires.

She would do anything to please me.

She had everything I was looking for in a woman, yet even when I tried, I couldn’t let my heart fall in love.

Why?

## **PART 8 — Nancy, Discipline, and the First Cracks in the Mask**

*(Text A authoritative; no Text B insertion yet)*

---

One thing that always amazed me about Nancy was her persistence and consistency in getting on her knees every night and every morning to pray and read her Bible.

She believed and had faith that the best was yet to come and that God had an extraordinary plan for our lives.

Her faithfulness in keeping a connection to God always brought a beam of hope into my heart, despite the shame and guilt that convinced me I didn’t deserve His mercy and grace because of my pride and selfishness that drove my choices.

---

During that time, I met a gentleman in New York. He was sitting across from me at a bar and kept staring at me.

After a while, I became annoyed and walked over to speak with him.

“Do I know you?” I asked.

“No,” he replied.

“Then why are you staring at me?”

He said, “I’m wondering what you’re all about.”

He offered me a drink, and we started talking.

He extended two fingers and said, “Squeeze as hard as you can.”

I grabbed his fingers and squeezed as hard as I could, hoping to break them.

He laughed and said, “You squeeze like a girl.”

Even though he was six-foot-four and weighed two hundred fifty pounds, I was ready to knock him out.

He quickly added, “I’m an eighth-degree black belt in Aikido and trained in Japan with Steven Seagal.”

I released his fingers and thought to myself, thank God I didn’t throw the first punch.

---

We spoke about our love for martial arts. He told me he owned a school in New Jersey and worked as an orthopedic doctor in New York.

I told him I wanted to restart martial arts training.

He invited me to meet him the next morning at the gym at 7:00 a.m., saying he would decide after one session whether I was worth training.

That night, I went to bed at 3:00 a.m. and set my alarm for 6:30 a.m.

I was not going to miss that opportunity.

I arrived at the gym ten minutes early.

To my surprise, he was already sitting at the juice bar waiting for me.

---

We went upstairs into a private room.

“Put your hands up and protect yourself,” he said.

I had never been hit or slapped so many times in my life.

His speed was lightning fast.

I went home with black eyes, swollen lips, and blood in my mouth.

After the session, he said, “I don’t think you have it in you.”

I replied, “What are you talking about? I never quit.”

“You’re right,” he said. “Same place, same time, two days from now.”

---

This sensei became a mentor to me.

We trained together for two years, and I earned my black belt under his guidance.

We opened a small school in New York and began training others in the art of Aikido.

He also became a confidant and coach.

He saw great potential in me and encouraged me to reconnect with the dreams and visions I had as a child.

He told me I needed to make radical decisions about my life because the path I was on would only grow darker.

---

I began searching for new business opportunities.

I came into contact with the president of a U.S. franchise called **Extreme Blendz**. They operated a healthy juice bar concept, one of which was located in the Gold’s Gym where I trained.

At that time, nothing like it existed in Canada.

A friend and I dreamed of taking the Canadian market by storm.

We invested a large amount of money and became the franchisor for Canada.

This required a one-hundred-eighty-degree change in direction — mindset, location, industry, and lifestyle.

---

This dream reignited a fire inside me.

Was this finally the exit from the poisonous, pitch-black darkness that filled my lungs, thoughts, and emotions every second of the day?

We moved back to Canada and opened our first location inside the new Florida Fitness gym in Ottawa.

We rented two beautiful condos in one of the most expensive high-rises in the city.

We worked eighty hours a week for the first six months.

## **PART 9 — Collapse of the Exit Plan and Return of the Night**

---

It didn’t take long to realize that to make a decent profit, we would need to open many locations and sell thousands of protein shakes per day.

Even then, it was nothing compared to what we had made in the nightlife industry.

I had sold myself the idea that by leaving New York, I was also leaving behind drugs and alcohol.

That belief was an illusion.

We had left Egypt, but Egypt was still living inside us.

---

Party nights returned.

Sleepless nights followed.

Hallucinations and fear began to consume me after savage nights of drugs and alcohol.

I kept promising myself that this would be the last time, only to find myself craving that numbness again — relief from anxiety, panic, and fear.

I was succumbing to another descent into hell.

---

Pride, the allure of an expensive lifestyle, the persona I had built, and the greed for more fame and money drove my decisions.

I chose to keep the penthouse in New York.

I asked Nancy to return there every two months to work and financially support our lifestyle until I “made it” with the business.

How selfish and egocentric I had become.

---

I did not realize that addiction was quietly taking root deep within me.

During that season, I met a man named John.

He opened a kickboxing and boxing club within the same fitness facility.

A strong friendship formed, and he became my kickboxing coach.

I believe God placed him in my life to bring light into my darkness.

He was inspiring and deeply knowledgeable in personal development.

He planted seeds of greatness in me — seeds that would take root later in my life.

---

Nancy had a deep, soul-level love for animals.

As a child, animals had helped her survive the cruelty of her upbringing.

They became her best friends and confidants.

There was a mutual, unconditional love between them.

---

I wanted to surprise her with a special Christmas gift.

I bought her a pug named Gucci.

Gucci brought immense joy and happiness into her life.

She became Nancy’s constant companion and emotional anchor.

Gucci would later become a therapeutic support during the trials and tests that awaited us in the next decade.

## **PART 10 — Betrayal, Collapse, and the Move to Toronto**

---

Just over a year into this journey in Ottawa, everything began to spiral downward.

A competitor opened its doors in Canada called Booster Juice. Their franchisees bought supplies from a Canadian manufacturer and franchisor, while our franchisees were required to purchase supplies from the United States at one of the highest exchange rates in the last twenty years.

The dream of franchising the concept quickly turned into a nightmare.

---

At the same time, the gym where we had opened our flagship location went under bankruptcy protection.

People were no longer coming in to buy memberships — they were canceling them.

Our revenue dropped by sixty percent overnight.

---

Simultaneously, my business partner went behind my back and began opening franchises through another corporation.

I had no choice but to buy him out.

---

I plunged into deep anger.

I felt betrayed, abandoned, rejected, and treated unjustly.

I remember crying out to God, “Please help me.”

Even though I had turned my back on Him, I kept Him as a spare tire — calling on His name whenever I felt myself hitting rock bottom.

---

In those moments, I feel trapped in a pit of darkness—physically, emotionally, and spiritually paralyzed. It’s like the light disappears completely. Yes, stress can play a role. # Wholness

### **A Simple Summary of What Unfolded**

Over the last few days, I’ve been moving through a deep state of **wholeness** — not as an idea, but as an embodied experience. It’s a felt sense that nothing is missing, nothing is excluded, and nothing needs to be fixed. A quiet fullness. Stillness. Peace. Presence.

After listening to an Alan Watts meditation, that word became very clear for me: **wholeness**. Not just oneness, not just union — but the inclusion of everything.

That morning, while stretching, I noticed two paintings on the wall.

The first was a **circle**. It immediately felt like what I was experiencing internally: no beginning, no end, no outside. Everything held. A symbol of union, communion, oneness, and wholeness — not static, but complete.

The second painting was an **X**. At first, it felt like my old life — confusion, multiple directions, effort, choosing without clarity. But when we talked about it together, something important landed:  
 the X doesn’t exist outside the circle — it lives **inside** it.

From the center of the X, you can move in any direction and never leave the wholeness of the circle. That shifted everything. It reframed choice, direction, and action as something that happens **within** wholeness, not away from it. Conviction without separation. Movement without fragmentation.

Later that same day, we met with the architect for the ViBUP Transform epicenter. When they showed the first sketch, I was stunned. Without knowing anything about my internal experience, they had designed a space with the **exact same structure**:  
 a large circular center, with pathways and movement radiating outward.

It wasn’t mystical — it was coherence. Inner clarity matching outer structure. It showed me that transformation isn’t meant to be chaotic or abstract, but **contained, grounded, and embodied**.

The following day, after an ibogaine booster, that sense of wholeness returned very strongly — felt in my body, my breath, my cells. And then, unexpectedly, I had to go for an MRI for my wrist.

Being in the MRI was intense: loud, confined, overwhelming — especially in a sensitive, post-medicine state. Fear, anger, and resistance came up. But instead of panicking or escaping, I stayed. I breathed. I remained present.

That moment reminded me that **wholeness doesn’t mean the absence of discomfort**. It means being able to include fear, anger, and stress without losing myself.

The real integration wasn’t the bliss — it was staying grounded under pressure.

What all of this showed me is simple and important:

Wholeness isn’t a peak state.  
 It’s the ability to live, choose, move, and face reality **without fragmenting**.

Nothing here feels like destiny or signs or something to chase.  
 It feels like alignment, maturity, and embodiment.

And right now, the work isn’t to analyze it further — it’s to let it settle into ordinary life, gently, quietly, and with presence.
