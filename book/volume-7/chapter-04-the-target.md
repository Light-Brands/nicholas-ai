# Chapter Four
## The Target

---

*"Successful Outside, Dying Inside"*

---

Let me describe the person I am called to serve.

They are everywhere. They are invisible. They are the most successful people in the room—and they are dying inside.

---

## The Successful Facade

From the outside, they have everything.

The career that others envy. The income that provides comfort. The marriage that looks perfect on social media. The children in the right schools. The body maintained by expensive trainers. The vacations documented for everyone to admire.

They speak at conferences. They lead teams. They make decisions that affect thousands. They are the success stories that coaches point to, that parents aspire to raise, that business schools feature in case studies.

They have arrived.

And they wake at 3 AM, unable to sleep, wondering why they feel so empty.

---

## The Hidden Reality

Behind the facade:

**The marriage is hollow.** They sleep in the same bed but haven't truly connected in years. There may have been affairs—or the constant temptation toward them. The partnership that looked so promising has become a business arrangement with occasional sex.

**The addiction is managed.** They're not falling-down drunk at business dinners. They're functional. But they need the drink to wind down. The porn to feel something. The buying to fill the void. The work itself to avoid what awaits at home.

**The mental health is crumbling.** Anxiety medicated. Depression medicated. The exhaustion attributed to the demands of success. They have a psychiatrist and a prescription but never the time for real therapy.

**The spiritual life is performance.** Maybe they attend church—it's good for appearances, for the children. But they haven't felt God in years. The prayers are rote. The faith is inherited rather than owned.

They are dying.

Not physically—not yet.

But something essential is fading. The spark that once drove them is replaced by grim endurance. They cannot remember the last time they felt joy.

---

## Why This Target?

These are my people because I was one of them.

VP of Sales. Seven-figure potential. Speaking engagements and industry recognition. And underneath: addiction, mental health crisis, marriage held together by Nancy's prayers and my performance.

I know this world from inside.

I know the pressure to appear invulnerable when you're crumbling.

I know the loneliness of success—surrounded by admirers but truly known by no one.

I know the exhaustion of maintaining the facade while fighting demons in private.

I know the shame of being the problem when you're supposed to be the solution.

These people are unreached by most ministry.

The church doesn't know how to help them—they're too successful to need help.

The therapists they see are often as professionally bound as they are.

The twelve-step rooms are foreign territory—too humble, too public, too confessional.

They suffer alone, in silence, behind walls of achievement.

Someone has to reach them.

I am someone.

---

## What They Need

These successful ones do not need more success strategies.

They do not need more leadership development.

They do not need more marriage tips or communication hacks.

They need transformation.

**They need to face the wounds they've been running from.**

The childhood trauma they buried under achievement. The rejection they've been medicating with accumulation. The shame they've been compensating for with performance.

**They need to break the covenants that drive them.**

"My worth equals my output." "Rest is for the weak." "Vulnerability is career suicide." The invisible agreements that fuel their success while destroying their souls.

**They need to reclaim identity that doesn't depend on achievement.**

Not the identity of VP or CEO or successful entrepreneur—the identity of son or daughter, loved before accomplishment, valuable apart from production.

**They need to rebuild their marriages from the ground up.**

Not patch the exterior—rebuild the foundation. Face the distance. Feel the pain they've caused and received. Learn to love authentically instead of perform intimacy.

**They need a community of peers who understand.**

Other successful people who have faced their shadow. Leaders who can speak the same professional language but have learned to speak a deeper human language.

This is what the epicenters will provide.

This is what most of the world cannot offer them.

---

## The Gatekeeping Problem

These successful ones are hard to reach.

They are surrounded by gatekeepers—assistants, lawyers, advisors. Their time is scheduled months out. Their access is controlled.

More than that, they gatekeep themselves.

Pride prevents vulnerability. "I don't need help. I am the helper." They advise others, mentor others, counsel others—but receiving help would be admission of weakness.

Fear prevents exposure. What if someone found out? The reputation, carefully cultivated, could be destroyed. The board. The investors. The colleagues. Everyone is watching.

Shame prevents confession. The gap between who they appear to be and who they actually are is so vast that bridging it feels impossible. Better to maintain the performance than face the truth.

**How do you reach people who are protected from being reached?**

Through relationship. Through trust. Through connection with others who have been where they are.

This is why transformed leaders become the primary evangelists for transformation. They can speak to peers in ways that outsiders cannot.

They can say: "I was where you are. I know the facade. I know the fear. And I found something that changed everything."

This word, peer to peer, is how the message penetrates the gatekeeping.

---

## The Stakes

What's at stake for these successful ones?

**Marriages.** Without intervention, many of these marriages will end in divorce—expensive, traumatic, damaging to children.

**Health.** The stress of sustained facade maintenance destroys bodies. Heart disease. Autoimmune conditions. Early death.

**Children.** What patterns are being passed down? What do children learn when parents are absent, stressed, emotionally unavailable?

**Legacy.** All the building, all the achieving—what does it mean if it ends in empty success and broken family?

The stakes are generational.

The wounds that are not healed will be inherited.

The transformation that happens in one leader ripples forward into everything they touch.

---

*Dear Traveler,*

*Do you know this person?*

*Perhaps you are this person.*

*Successful outside, dying inside. Admired by many, known by none. The achiever who cannot rest, the leader who cannot lead their own soul.*

*If this is you—there is another way.*

*The success you've built does not have to collapse. But it needs a foundation of genuine wholeness underneath it.*

*There is transformation available that does not require abandoning your achievements.*

*It requires facing them honestly.*

*The facade can come down.*

*The real you can emerge.*

*And you might discover that who you actually are is more valuable than who you've been performing.*
